jjajangmyeon

="jjajangmyeon.jpg">

Jjajangmyeon is everybody’s favorite food. When I was young, a plate of jjajangmyeon from a Chinese restaurant always made me excited. When you order it from a Chinese restaurant they bring you the noodles in a special tin box in under 30 minutes!

  • noodles
  • 1/2 pound pork belly
  • 1 cup korean radish
  • 1 cup potato
  • 3 tbs vegetable oil
  • 1 tbs and 1/4 cup black bean paste
  • water
  1. Stir-fry the pork belly in a large, deep wok with 1 Tablespoon of vegetable oil for about 4-5 minutes, until golden brown and crispy.
  2. Pour out the excess pork fat. Add radish and stir fry for 1 minute.
  3. Add potato, onion, and zucchini and keep stirring for about 3 minutes until the potato looks a little translucent. Clear a space in the center of the wok by pushing the ingredients to the edges.
  4. Add 2 Tablespoons of vegetable oil to the center of the wok, then add ¼ cup of black bean paste and stir it with a wooden spoon for 1 minute to fry it. Then mix everything in the wok and keep stirring.
